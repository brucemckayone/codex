The five stages

There is a way down. It begins at the root.

Rootwork is a descent — five stages the body walks to find its ground again. You don't climb toward calm. You settle into it.

  1. i

    Regulation

    Finding the ground. Teaching the body it is safe enough to soften.

  2. ii

    Embodiment

    Coming back into sensation — feeling what is actually here.

  3. iii

    Attunement

    Listening inward; letting need and impulse be known.

  4. iv

    Co-regulation

    Being met by another. The body settling in company.

  5. v

    Reclamation

    Living from wholeness — taking back what was held away.

    Before you begin

    The honest answers.

    I've never done somatic work. Is that a problem?

    Not at all — Rootwork assumes nothing. The earliest practices are the gentlest, and the order is built to hold your hand the whole way down.

    What if I can't feel anything in my body?

    That's common, and it's welcome. Numbness is information too — several of the practices are designed to begin exactly there, with patience.

    How much time does it take?

    Most practices run 6–12 minutes. Ten minutes a day is plenty; the work is in the returning, not the length.

    Is this therapy?

    No. Rootwork is a practice, not treatment — a companion alongside support, never a replacement for it.
